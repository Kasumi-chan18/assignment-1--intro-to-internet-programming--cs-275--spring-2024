Ten Principles for Good Design

1. Innovative 2. Useful 3. Aesthetic 4. Understandable 5. Unobtrusive 6. Honest 7. Long-lasting 8. Detail-oriented 9. Environmentally-friendly 10. Minimalistic

1. Innovative

The possiblities for progression is not exhausted, and design always develops with improving technology.

2. Useful

A product should satisfy functional, psychological, and aesthetic criteria, emphasizing its usefulness.

3. Aesthetic

The aesthetic quality of a product is integral to its usefulness, affecting people's well-being.

4. Understandable

Good design clarifies the product's structure and function, making it self-explanatory.

5. Unobtrusive

Products fulling a purpose should have a neutral and restrained design to leave room for user expression.

6. Honest

Good design does not make false promises or manipulate consumers.

7. Long-lasting

It avoids being fashionable and last many years, even in today's throwaway society.

8. Detail-oriented

Design should be thorough down to the last detail, showing respect towards the consumer.

9. Environmentally-friendly

Good design conserves resources and minimizes pollution throughout the product's lifecycle.

10. Minimalistic

Less, but better - focusing on essential aspects without burdening the product with non-essentials.
